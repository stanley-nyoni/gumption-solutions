Common dishwasher faults explained

Understanding what is wrong helps you make an informed decision about repair. Here is what our Cape Town dishwasher technicians encounter most often.

Dishwasher Not Draining

Standing water at the bottom of your dishwasher after a cycle is one of the most common faults we repair in Cape Town. The usual causes are a blocked filter, a failed drain pump motor, a kinked or blocked drain hose, or a faulty check valve. We clear the blockage or replace the failed component, often on the same visit.

Dishwasher Not Cleaning Properly

If your dishes are coming out dirty, greasy, or with food residue, the problem is usually blocked or cracked spray arms preventing proper water distribution, a failing heating element that cannot reach wash temperature, a water inlet valve not letting enough water in, or a detergent dispenser that is not releasing soap at the right time.

Dishwasher Leaking

A dishwasher leak can come from a deteriorated door gasket, a cracked spray arm, a faulty water inlet valve, a loose or cracked hose connection, or a worn pump seal. Leaks left unattended cause water damage to your flooring and cabinetry. We identify the exact source and fix it properly.

Dishwasher Not Drying

Dishes that come out wet are usually the result of a faulty heating element, a broken thermostat that prevents the drying cycle from reaching temperature, or a malfunctioning rinse aid dispenser. On some modern dishwashers, a failing vent fan is the cause. We diagnose and fix drying faults across all major brands.

Error Codes and Control Board Faults

Bosch E15, Samsung LC, Defy and Whirlpool error codes all point to specific component faults. Our technicians are trained to interpret and resolve error codes across all dishwasher brands rather than simply clearing the display. We fix the underlying fault to prevent the code from returning.

Noisy Dishwashers

A dishwasher that makes grinding, buzzing, or rattling sounds during a cycle usually has a failing circulation pump, a loose spray arm hitting the inside of the unit, or debris caught in the pump. We diagnose noise faults and replace worn pump components and spray arms across all brands.
